Introduction

Africabima Limited (“Africabima”, “us”, “our” or “we”) is committed to safeguarding your privacy. This Privacy Policy applies to all users of our website, the Africabima mobile application (“App”), and any services provided by Africabima through the Website and/or App (the “Services”). It explains the types of information we collect, how we use it to deliver our Services, and how we strive to give you a secure and improved user experience.

This Privacy Policy forms part of, and is incorporated into, our Terms & Conditions. By using our Website, App, or Services, you agree to the practices described in this Policy and consent to our collection, storage, use, and disclosure of your information as outlined here.

1. Definitions and Interpretation

For the purposes of this Privacy Policy, the following definitions apply:

  • Applicable Law: The Constitution of the Republic of Kenya, all Acts of Parliament including regulations, rules, guidelines, guidance notes issued pursuant to any Act of Parliament, legislative and regulatory requirements, and codes of practice applicable to the processing of personal data and/or applicable to a data controller or data processor, as may be amended from time to time.
  • Personal Data: Any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person (“Data Subject”). An identifiable person is one who can be identified, directly or indirectly, in particular by reference to an identifier such as a name, identification number, location data, an online identifier, or factors specific to the physical, physiological, genetic, mental, economic, cultural, or social identity of that person.
  • Controller: A natural or legal person, authority, organization, or other agency that determines, individually or jointly with others, the purposes and means of processing personal data.
  • Africabima: Includes all subsidiaries and affiliates of Africabima (hereafter referred to as “Africabima”, “we”, “us” or “our”).
  • Processing: Any operation or set of operations performed on personal data, whether or not by automated means.
  • Processor: A natural or legal person, authority, organization, or other agency that processes personal data on behalf of the Controller.
  • Sub-processor: A contractual partner of the Processor, engaged to carry out specific processing activities on behalf of the Processor.
  • Third Party: Any natural or legal person, public authority, agency, or body other than the Data Subject, Controller, Processor, Sub-processor, and persons who, under the direct authority of the Controller or Processor, are authorized to process personal data.
  • Website: The official Africabima website, accessible at https://africabima.com.
  • Online and Mobile Platform Services: Insurance services offered by Africabima through its online and mobile platforms.

In addition to the definitions above, unless the context requires otherwise:

  • Definitions of terms in our general Terms & Conditions also apply to this Policy.
  • The singular includes the plural and vice versa.
  • A reference to any gender (masculine, feminine, neuter) includes the others.
  • Headings and sub-headings are for convenience only and shall not affect interpretation.

2. The Data We Collect

We may collect, use, store, and transfer different kinds of Personal Data about you, which we have grouped as follows:

  • Identity Data: Includes name, username or similar identifier, Identity Card/Passport number, PIN number, photo, marital status, property details (relevant for insurance), family details (such as names of your children and parents), fingerprints, race, nationality, ethnic or social origin, color, age, title, date of birth, gender, and any other similar information.
  • Contact Data: Includes billing address, postal address, physical address, email address, and telephone numbers.
  • Financial Data: Includes bank account details, card payment details, and other electronic or non-electronic payment details (relevant for premiums/claims).
  • Transaction Data: Includes details about payments to and from you, and details of products and services you have acquired from us.
  • Technical Data: Includes Internet Protocol (IP) address, login identity data, browser type and version, time zone setting and location, browser plug-in types and versions, device information, operating system and platform, and other technology on the devices you use to access our systems.
  • Profile Data: Includes your profile identification information, purchases or orders made by you, your interests, preferences, feedback, and survey responses.
  • Usage Data: Includes information about how you use our website, products, and services.
  • Marketing & Communications Data: Includes your preferences in receiving marketing information from us and our third parties, as well as your communication preferences.
  • Visitor Data: Includes personal information/identification details of visitors on our premises (if applicable).
  • Biometric Data: Includes fingerprints, images, voice, and other similar information, as well as surveillance footage captured by CCTV cameras on our premises (if applicable).
  • Employment Data: Includes employment history and educational background (relevant for certain insurance products).

We may also collect, use, and share Aggregated Data such as statistical or demographic data for any purpose. Aggregated Data may be derived from your Personal Data but is not considered Personal Data in law as this data will not directly or indirectly reveal your identity. For example, we may aggregate your Usage Data to calculate the percentage of users accessing a specific feature of our Services.

6. How We Use Cookies on Our Website

We may place electronic “cookies” in the browser files of your computer when you access our Website. Cookies are small pieces of information that allow our systems to recognize your browser and tailor content on our Website to your interests.

For example, if you previously visited our Website and inquired about certain services, cookies allow us to present information tailored to your account or interests on your next visit. Additionally, Africabima, or our trusted third-party service providers and business partners, may place cookies on your device that can be matched to other personal information we maintain about you—helping us pre-populate online forms for your convenience.

We also use cookies to analyze how visitors interact with our Website. This helps us identify which areas of our site are most useful and popular, enabling us to improve and update the Website more effectively.

Managing Cookies

Most web browsers are set to accept cookies by default. However, you can change your browser settings to either reject cookies or notify you when a cookie is about to be placed on your computer. Please note that rejecting cookies may cause some features or sections of our Website to function less efficiently. For more details, please refer to our Cookie Policy.

11. How We Keep Your Information Secure

Africabima has implemented appropriate technical and organizational security measures to protect your Personal Data from loss, misuse, unauthorized access, alteration, or disclosure. These measures are designed to maintain the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of your data.

Access to your Personal Data is strictly limited to employees, agents, contractors, and other third parties who have a legitimate business need to know. Such parties are only permitted to process your data in accordance with Africabima’s instructions and are bound by strict confidentiality obligations.

Africabima also maintains procedures to promptly address any suspected personal data breach. Where legally required, we will notify both you and the relevant regulatory authority of any breach that could compromise your Personal Data.

12. How Long We Shall Retain Your Personal Data

Africabima will retain your Personal Data only for as long as is reasonably necessary to fulfill the purposes for which it was collected, including to meet legal, regulatory, tax, accounting, or reporting requirements. We may also retain your data for a longer period if required to resolve a complaint or if litigation is reasonably anticipated in relation to our business relationship with you.

When determining the appropriate retention period, we consider the nature and sensitivity of the Personal Data, the amount of data held, the potential risks associated with unauthorized access or disclosure, the purposes for which the data is processed, and applicable legal and regulatory requirements.

By law, Africabima must retain certain basic customer information (including contact, identity, financial, and transaction data) for a minimum of seven (7) years after a customer ceases to use our services. In line with internal policies, we may be required to keep customer data for longer periods where necessary.

In some cases, Africabima may anonymize your Personal Data so that it can no longer be associated with you. Such anonymized data may be retained indefinitely and used for research, statistical analysis, or other legitimate business purposes without further notice to you.

13. Data Subject’s Rights

Subject to legal and contractual exceptions, you have rights under applicable laws in relation to your Personal Data. These include:

  • Right to be informed – to know that we are collecting your personal information and how it is being processed.
  • Right to rectification – to have your personal data corrected where it is inaccurate or incomplete.
  • Right to withdraw consent – to withdraw consent to the processing of your personal data. However, we may continue processing where there are legitimate interests or legal grounds.
  • Right to object – to object to processing of all or part of your personal data. Africabima may decline such a request if we are legally obliged or entitled to continue processing.
  • Right of erasure – to request deletion of your personal data held by us, noting that we may continue to retain your information if legally required or entitled to do so.
  • Right of access – to access your personal data in our possession.
  • Right against profiling – to not be subjected to profiling or automated decision-making regarding your personal data, unless legally permitted.
  • Right to restriction – to request restricted processing of your personal data. Africabima may continue processing if legally obliged or entitled to do so.
  • Right to data portability – to request your personal data in a portable format (e.g., electronic file).

Exercising Your Rights

Africabima is committed to ensuring that you can easily exercise your data protection rights in compliance with applicable regulations. You may make a request by:

  • Submitting a request via the Contact Us page.
  • Completing and submitting the Data Subject Rights Request Form (downloadable form – placeholder link).
  • Contacting our Data Protection Officer (DPO) using the contact details provided in this Policy.

If you believe that Africabima has not adequately addressed your data protection concerns, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the appropriate regulatory authority. Our DPO will also guide you through the complaint process if needed.

To protect your information, we may request additional details to confirm your identity and ensure your right to access your Personal Data (or exercise other rights). This is a security measure to prevent unauthorized access. We may also contact you to clarify or speed up your request.

Africabima aims to respond to all legitimate requests within a reasonable time. However, for particularly complex requests or multiple requests, additional time may be required. In such cases, we will notify you and keep you updated on the progress.
